Saving Food Awareness

Learn how to save food and reduce waste with these simple tips and tutorials.

How to Tell If Bread is Still Edible

Bread is a staple food in many households, but it can quickly go stale or moldy if not stored properly. Here’s how to determine if your bread is still good to eat:

  • Check for mold: Look for green or black spots. If you see any, discard the bread.
  • Smell it: If it smells sour or off, it’s no longer safe to eat.
  • Feel the texture: Stale bread is dry and hard but still edible if there’s no mold. You can revive it by heating it slightly in an oven.

Pro Tip: Store your bread in a cool, dry place or freeze it to extend its shelf life.

How to Check If Milk is Still Good

Milk is another commonly wasted food item. Use these tips to check if your milk is still drinkable:

  • Smell it: Fresh milk has a neutral smell. If it smells sour or unpleasant, it has likely spoiled.
  • Check the texture: If the milk appears lumpy or curdled, it is no longer safe to consume.
  • Taste a small amount: If unsure, taste a tiny amount. If it tastes sour, don’t consume it.

Pro Tip: Store milk at or below 4°C (40°F) to keep it fresh longer.
